LIFE EXPECTANCY

Life expectancy is the number of years an individual can
expect to live.

The data in this chosen dataset was collected from the
Global Health Observatory (GHO) data repository under
the World Health Organization (WHO) and from United
Nation website.

This dataset contains data from year 2000 to 2015 for 193
countries for further analysis.

All the predicting variables in this dataset had been divided
into several broad categories, such as Immunization related factors, mortality factors, economical factors, and also social factors.

DISTRIBUTION OF COUNTRIES IN BETWEEN DIFFERENT CONTINENTS AND AVERAGE HEALTH STATUSES BASED
ON BMI

Question may arise such as what is the impact of BMI status on the life expectancy of an
individual?

Before that, let us have an overview on the distribution of countries based on their average
BMI status across different continents.

We can clearly note that the average BMI status of all European, North and South American
countries appeaerd to be Obese. It tells that for every person in those countries, we may
expect that he/she was an obese person.


INTERACTIVE PLOT SHOWING RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN SCHOOLING DURATION WITH BMI ACROSS DIFFERENT
CONTINENTS AND TRENDS OVER TIME

Furthermore, does schooling duration affect the BMI status of an individual?

According to the scatter plot shown below, the answer is apparently a Yes. There seemed to
have moderate to strong positive correlation between average schooling duration with
average BMI. This statement could be supported by Abrams, Cohen, Rai, & Rehkopf (2013).

From 2000 to 2015, the average schooling duration worldwide had been gradually increasing.
Meanwhile, Australia had constantly be the country that had the highest average schooling
duration over the 16 years.


ASSOCIATION BETWEEN ALCOHOL PER CAPITA CONSUMPTION, BMI AND LIFE EXPECTANCY

We find out that BMI was slightly or moderately associated with alcohol consumption.
However, from the bubble plot, it was visible that when the alcohol per capita consumption
was too high (more than 10 litres/capita), then the BMI status was always an Obese.

Other than that, we realize that both alcohol consumption and BMI did not seem to affect
the life expectancy, as the the big and small-sized bubbles were scattered everywhere and
did not really gather at any position in the plot. So, no clear pattern was displayed.

From the dash plot, we learn that a large proportion of the population worldwide was obese.


CHANGES IN THE WORLD'S AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY,
INFANT DEATH RATE AND SCHOOLING DURATION FROM YEAR 2000 TO 2015

Last but not least, we might also be curious about the correlation between infant death rate
and schooling duration with the average life expectancy.

As indicated below, from 2000 to 2015, the world's average life expectancy and average schooling
duration were constantly improving. Following this, we discover that lower infant death rate and
higher schooling duration could lead to higher average life expectancy.

In summary, since we know that high schooling duration brought to high BMI and high average life expectancy, hence we could probably deduce that obesity was not a culprit of low life expectancy.
